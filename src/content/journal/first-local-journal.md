# Mentoring at the SIM–LSE Data Analytics Challenge 2026

One of the highlights of 2026 was having the opportunity to co-design and mentor the **SIM–LSE Data Analytics Challenge 2026**, a regional data analytics competition organized by **SIM Global Education** and the **London School of Economics and Political Science (LSE)**.

Together with my teammate, I designed and delivered a **four-week mentoring program** that introduced high school students to the fundamentals of data analytics. The program covered business problem solving, data storytelling, and Tableau, culminating in students developing their own analytical insights for the competition.

What impressed me most wasn't just how quickly the students learned Tableau—it was their curiosity, willingness to ask thoughtful questions, and confidence in tackling completely new challenges. Many participants were still in **high school**, yet they approached data analytics with remarkable enthusiasm and creativity.

As a **data engineer**, I spend most of my time building reliable data pipelines and infrastructure. Mentoring this program reminded me that data is ultimately about enabling better decisions and communicating insights effectively. It was a refreshing opportunity to step outside my usual role and help others discover the value of working with data.

I'm grateful to **SIM**, **LSE**, my **fellow mentor**, and all of the **students** for making this such a rewarding experience. I look forward to contributing to more educational initiatives that inspire the next generation of data professionals.
